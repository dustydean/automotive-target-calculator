📚 About the Marketing % Benchmark

The 10% default comes from NADA (National Automobile Dealers Association) industry data, which shows dealerships typically allocate 6-10% of gross profit to total advertising and marketing (NADA 2023-24, Haig Partners).

What this covers:

  • Digital channels: 70-75% of budget (Google, Meta, Cars.com, Autotrader, etc.)
  • Traditional media: TV, radio, billboards, print (~25-30% of budget)
  • Other marketing: Sponsorships, events, direct mail

Typical allocations:

  • Conservative/profitable: 6-8% (focus on efficiency)
  • Moderate/balanced: 8-10% (balanced growth and profit)

🎯 How to Implement Your Targets in Ad Platforms

Now that you know your target CPA of $1,200, here's how to use it in Google Ads and Meta to automatically control costs and scale efficiently:

🔍 Google Ads: Target CPA Bidding

What it is: An automated bid strategy that sets bids to get as many conversions as possible at your target cost per acquisition.

How it works:

  • Google's algorithm adjusts your bids in real-time based on the likelihood of conversion
  • It considers device, location, time of day, and user signals to optimize each auction
  • Bids higher when a conversion is more likely, lower when it's less likely
  • Over time, it learns which users are most likely to convert and prioritizes them

How to set it up:

  1. In Google Ads, go to Settings → Bidding
  2. Select "Target CPA" as your bid strategy
  3. Enter your target: $1,200 (your calculated target from above)
  4. Google recommends at least 30 conversions in the last 30 days for best performance

Benefits for scalability:

  • Automatic cost control: Google won't consistently overspend your target (though individual conversions may vary)
  • Scales with you: As you increase budget, it finds more conversions at the same target CPA
  • Saves time: No manual bid adjustments needed - the algorithm optimizes 24/7
  • Adapts to seasonality: Automatically adjusts for market changes and competition shifts

📚 Learn more: Google Ads Target CPA Bidding Guide

📱 Meta Ads: Cost Per Result Goal

What it is: A bid control that tells Meta's algorithm the maximum amount you want to pay per conversion (lead, purchase, etc.) on average.

How it works:

  • Meta's delivery system focuses on showing ads to people likely to convert at or below your cost goal
  • It uses machine learning based on past conversion data from your Pixel/Conversions API
  • The system enters auctions where it can meet your cost goal and skips auctions that would exceed it
  • Balances between volume and cost efficiency automatically

How to set it up:

  1. When creating a campaign, choose your conversion objective (Leads, Sales, etc.)
  2. In Ad Set settings, go to "Optimization & Delivery"
  3. Under "Cost per result goal," toggle it ON
  4. Enter your target: $1,200 per conversion
  5. Meta recommends at least 50 conversions per week for optimal performance

Benefits for scalability:

  • Volume with guardrails: Meta tries to maximize conversions while respecting your cost ceiling
  • Testing efficiency: You can test new audiences/creatives knowing costs won't spiral
  • Budget flexibility: Increase budget and Meta will find more conversions at your target cost
  • Prevents overspending: If Meta can't achieve your cost goal, it will under-deliver budget rather than exceed your target

📚 Learn more: Meta Cost Per Result Goal Guide

💡 Pro Strategy: Let the Algorithms Do the Heavy Lifting

Instead of manually managing bids across keywords, audiences, and placements, modern automated bidding strategies (like Target CPA and Cost Per Result Goal) use machine learning trained on billions of signals to optimize in real-time.

Your role shifts from bid management to strategy:

  • Set the right target CPA (you just calculated it: $1,200)
  • Feed the algorithms with quality conversion data (proper tracking setup)
  • Test new audiences, ad creative, and messaging
  • Monitor performance and adjust targets as profitability changes

Result: More conversions, less manual work, controlled costs, and easy scalability.
